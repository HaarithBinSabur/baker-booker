English Muffin

English muffins are these delightful little round bread discs that originated in England. They're unique in that they're typically split in half, creating a perfect nook for all kinds of toppings.
The nooks and crannies are iconic in English muffins. This texture is achieved by cooking the dough on a griddle, creating a slightly crispy exterior while keeping the inside soft and fluffy. Sour Dough

Sourdough is a stable culture of lactic acid bacteria and yeast in a micture of flour and water.Brodly speaking the yeast produces gas(carbon dioxide) which leavens the dough, and the lactic acid bacteria produce lactic acid, which contributes flavor in the form of sourness.
One of the oldest Sourdough breads dated back to the 1300 BCE and excavated in Switzerland, but the origin of sourdough fermentation likely relates to the origin of agriculture in the Fertile cresent and Egypt several thousand years earlier.

Bagel

Bagels is a bread roll origianted in the Jewish communities of poland. Bagels are traditionaly made from yeasted wheat dough that is shaped by hand into a torus or ring, briefly boiled in water and then baked. The result is a dense, chewy, doughy interior with a browned and sometimes crisp exterior.
The Earliest known mention of a boiled-then-baked ring-shaped bread can be found in a 13th-century Syrian cookbook, where they referred to ka'ak.

Focaccia

Focaccia is a flat leavened oven-baked Italian bread. In some Contemporary places, such as rome, it is a style of pizza, also called pizza bianca (white pizza). The shape maybe round, rectangular, or a square shape.
The first attenstation of the word focaccia dates back to the 14th century.Sometimes it is considerd to be a variant of pizza, although focaccia is left to rise after being flattend, while pizza is baked immediately.

Corn Bread

Cornbread is a quick bread made with cornmeal, associated with the cuisine of the Southern United States, with origins in Native American cuisine. It is an example of batter bread.
Native people in the Americas began using corn (maize) and ground corn as food thousands of years before Europeans arrived in the New World. First domesticated in Mexico around six thousand years ago, corn was introduced to what is now the United States between three thousand and one thousand years ago.

Pumpernickel Bread

Pimpernickel is a typically dense, slightly sweet rye bread traditionaly made with sourdough starter and coarsely ground rye. it is sometimes made with a combination of rye flour and whole rye grains.
The word supposedly stems from an old Bavarian term for "hard", either referring to the process used to grind the grain into flour, or the density of the final bread product.
